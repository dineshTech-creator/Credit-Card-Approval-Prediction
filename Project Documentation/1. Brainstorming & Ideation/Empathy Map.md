# Empathy Map
## CrediShield AI — Credit Card Approval Prediction System

---

## Target User Persona

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Name** | Priya Sharma |
| **Role** | Junior Credit Analyst at a mid-size NBFC |
| **Age** | 26 years |
| **Experience** | 2 years in credit operations |
| **Location** | Hyderabad, India |
| **Education** | B.Com with MBA in Finance |
| **Daily Workload** | Reviews 20–30 credit applications per day |
| **Tools Used** | Excel, Calculator, Paper files, Email |

---

## Empathy Map Canvas

### 👁️ SEES (What does Priya observe in her environment?)

- Piles of pending credit card application files on her desk every morning
- Excel spreadsheets with hundreds of rows of applicant data
- Colleagues manually cross-referencing payment histories with credit bureau reports
- Monthly rejection-rate reports posted on the office notice board by management
- Newspaper articles and LinkedIn posts about banks adopting AI for lending decisions
- Senior analysts spending 30+ minutes on each borderline application
- Customers waiting in the branch lobby to check their application status
- Dashboard screens in the office showing pending application counts climbing daily

---

### 👂 HEARS (What does Priya hear from others?)

- **From her manager**: "We need to process applications faster — the backlog is growing."
- **From colleagues**: "I'm not sure about this applicant. Can someone double-check?"
- **From the news**: "Top banks are using AI to approve credit cards in under 60 seconds."
- **From HR**: "There's a new training session on RBI's updated lending guidelines next week."
- **From customers**: "It's been 2 weeks since I applied. What's taking so long?"
- **From senior analysts**: "Trust your instincts, but always check the debt-to-income ratio."
- **From the IT team**: "We're planning to digitize the application process by next quarter."
- **From industry conferences**: "Machine learning can predict default risk with 95%+ accuracy."

---

### 💭 THINKS & FEELS (What goes on in Priya's mind?)

**Thoughts:**
- "I wish I had a tool that could instantly tell me if this applicant is risky or safe."
- "Am I being too lenient? What if this person defaults and I get blamed?"
- "Am I being too strict? What if I'm rejecting genuinely good applicants?"
- "There has to be a more scientific way to do this than just gut feeling."
- "My senior approved someone similar last week — but rejected this one. What's the logic?"

**Feelings:**
- 😟 **Worried** about making wrong approval decisions that lead to defaults (NPAs)
- 😩 **Frustrated** by repetitive, manual data entry and cross-referencing work
- 🤞 **Hopeful** about upskilling in data analytics and ML to advance her career
- 😰 **Anxious** about meeting daily processing quotas set by management
- 😐 **Indifferent** about the outdated tools she's forced to use

---

### 🗣️ SAYS & DOES (What are Priya's observable actions?)

**Says:**
- "Can someone double-check this application? I'm not confident about the risk level."
- "I've processed 15 applications today, but I still have 12 more in the queue."
- "The income looks fine, but the employment history is concerning."
- "I wish we had a scoring system instead of relying on judgment calls."
- "Let me ask the senior analyst before I approve this borderline case."

**Does:**
- Manually calculates debt-to-income ratios using a desk calculator
- Copies data from handwritten application forms into Excel spreadsheets
- Cross-references credit bureau reports with application details
- Asks senior analysts for guidance on borderline/ambiguous cases
- Stays late 2–3 days per week to clear the application backlog
- Maintains a personal Excel tracker of her daily approvals/rejections
- Attends monthly training sessions on new compliance regulations

---

### 😣 PAIN POINTS (What frustrates Priya?)

| # | Pain Point | Severity |
|---|-----------|----------|
| 1 | **No standardized scoring system** — decisions rely on experience and gut feeling | 🔴 Critical |
| 2 | **30+ minutes per application** on manual verification and cross-referencing | 🔴 Critical |
| 3 | **Inconsistent criteria** — different analysts approve/reject identical profiles differently | 🟡 High |
| 4 | **High-risk applicants slip through** during peak workload periods due to analyst fatigue | 🟡 High |
| 5 | **Gets blamed** when approved clients subsequently default on payments | 🟡 High |
| 6 | **No visual dashboard** to track daily/weekly approval trends and team performance | 🟢 Medium |
| 7 | **Paper-based reports** that are difficult to search and analyze retrospectively | 🟢 Medium |
| 8 | **No career growth path** without learning modern tools like ML and analytics | 🟢 Medium |

---

### 🎯 GAINS (What would make Priya's life better?)

| # | Desired Gain | How CrediShield AI Addresses It |
|---|-------------|--------------------------------|
| 1 | **Instant approve/reject recommendation** for each application | Sub-second ML prediction via the web dashboard |
| 2 | **Confidence scores** that quantify risk numerically | Animated SVG gauge showing 0–100% confidence |
| 3 | **Risk grades** (Low/Medium/High) for quick categorization | Automated classification based on probability thresholds |
| 4 | **Printable assessment reports** for compliance and audit records | PDF "Credit Assessment Letter" generation with full details |
| 5 | **Visual analytics** showing team performance and portfolio trends | Chart.js dashboards (feature importance, approval ratios, demographics) |
| 6 | **Consistent, unbiased decisions** across all applications | Same trained model applied to every applicant identically |
| 7 | **More time for complex cases** that genuinely need human expert judgment | AI handles obvious cases; analyst focuses on borderline ones |
| 8 | **Career advancement** through exposure to modern ML-powered tools | Working with an AI system builds Priya's data literacy |

---

## Key Insight

> Priya doesn't need the AI to **replace** her — she needs it to **handle the obvious cases instantly** so she can focus her expertise on the borderline ones. The system must be **trustworthy** (showing confidence scores), **explainable** (showing risk levels and feature importance), and **fast** (sub-second predictions).

---

## How CrediShield AI Addresses Each Quadrant

| Empathy Quadrant | CrediShield AI Response |
|-----------------|------------------------|
| **SEES** (piles of applications) | Digital form replaces paper; instant results |
| **HEARS** ("process faster") | Sub-second predictions; no backlogs |
| **THINKS** ("is this risky?") | Confidence % and risk grade answer this objectively |
| **SAYS** ("double-check this") | AI provides the "second opinion" consistently |
| **PAINS** (30 min per app) | Reduces to 2 minutes (data entry + instant result) |
| **GAINS** (career growth) | Priya learns to work with ML-powered decision support |
